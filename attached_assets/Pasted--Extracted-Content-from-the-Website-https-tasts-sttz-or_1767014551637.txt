## About the Speaker

Dr. Gileard Minja holds a Ph.D. in Environmental Studies from the University of Adelaide in South Australia; a Master's degree (MSc) in Natural Resources Assessment and Management from the University of Dar-es-salam (Tanzania) and a BEd(Sc) Biology and Geography at the St. Augustine University of Tanzania. For the past 10 years, Dr. Gileard has been involved in teaching, research and consultancy. He has been lecturing in areas not limited to Tourism and Environment, Natural Resources Management, Geography of Energy, Environmental Resources and Food Security, Geography of Marketing, Bio-geography, Environmental Education and Conservation, Water Resources Management, Medical Geography, Regional Planning, Project Planning, Research Methods, and Environmental Disaster Management. He has also been working as a researcher in the natural resources field at Mwenge University in Kilimanjaro Tanzania. Dr. Gileard's main research interests are related to all aspects climate change and environment, community-based wildlife conservation, ecotourism, natural resources management, decision-making, policy issues, tourism, and service-learning. Other research interests include climate change, biodiversity conservation, biogeography, sustainable tourism development strategies, water resources, mineral resources, food security and environmental impacts. He has published (and co-published) in academic peer-reviewed journals and presented/authored papers at local and international conferences.

## About the Speaker

Dr. Nyanjige Mayala is a senior lecturer in the Department of Economics and Business Studies at Mwenge Catholic University in Tanzania. She has a PhD in Investment Analysis from Sokoine University of Agriculture and Moshi Co-operative University, an MBA, and a Bachelor of Commerce and Management from the University of Dar es Salaam. She has more than 15 years of experience in the field, working with local and international projects in some parts of Africa and Tanzania, and more than 10 years as an instructor in higher learning institutions in Tanzania. She has contributed to several Book Projects, including Fast-Tracking Industrialization in Tanzania - The Must-Do Actions (2020) in the tourism segment (pp. 261 - 284). # Day Trips in Moshi

### Main Content
**1.** **Materuni waterfalls and Coffee experience**

A day trip to **Materuni Waterfalls** is a refreshing blend of culture, adventure, and nature. Just a short drive from Moshi, the journey takes you through lush banana and coffee plantations, where locals warmly welcome you with stories of their traditions. The path winds through green hills and tropical vegetation until reaching the 90-meter waterfall, which reveals itself, cascading into a cool natural pool. After a guided waterfall tour, you will dive into coffee making, just learning traditional coffee production, harvesting, and processing until you make your own cup of coffee. You will enjoy the traditional lunch served in the local buffet before dropping off in Moshi town.

**2.** **Chemka Hot Spring**

The **Chemka Hot Springs** , also known as Kikuletwa, offer a completely different kind of magic. Hidden in a grove of fig trees, this turquoise oasis feels like a secret paradise in the middle of the savannah. The warm, crystal-clear water is ideal for swimming, floating, or simply soaking under the shade as you unwind from your safari or climb. Tiny fish nibble at your toes, adding a playful touch, while rope swings invite the adventurous to plunge into the pool with a splash. With picnic spots and plenty of time to relax, Chemka is less about the rush of exploration and more about slowing down, reconnecting with nature, and sharing laughter with fellow travelers.
